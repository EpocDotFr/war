About

Web Audio Recognizer (WAR) is a simple web page that allow you to recognize unknown music tracks and get the associated artist and title. Think about a Chazam, but web-based.

You can either submit an audio file or capture / sing the music via your microphone, or even capture the music currently playing from your speakers (a little bit of configuration is needed for this, see in the FAQ).

Author and genesis

WAR was created by Epoc. He primarly created it for himself, because he was frustated that Digitally Imported - a great web radio he listen for years - often play tracks that are mixes. Since mixes are several tracks mixed together in one huge track, you can't get the real tracks name.

As he uses several methods at several places to listen to DI, he wanted a system that didn't need to install anything (other than a modern web browser).

He also needed that this system searches several audio databases in one time to maximize the chances to get positive matches.

Technologies

WAR is rolling on Python (Flask) and Nginx. It also uses Beanstalk, Supervisor and Pusher.

It request the following audio databases for each requests:

  • AcoustID (complete audio files only)
  • ACRCloud
